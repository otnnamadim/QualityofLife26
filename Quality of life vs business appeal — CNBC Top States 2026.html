Is there a trade-off between business appeal and quality of life?

Each dot is a state; both axes are CNBC category ranks with better positioned up and to the right. The overall business ranking shows no relationship with quality of life — but the cost of doing business does. The Texas/Tennessee pattern is real, and it's a cost story, not a business-appeal story.

Quality of life vs overall business rank

Quality of life vs cost of doing business

Spearman ρ = −0.52 (p = 0.0001) — significant trade-off

Ranks from CNBC's 2026 America's Top States for Business study (1 = best of 50). Spearman rank correlation used because ranks are ordinal. Note that quality of life is itself a weighted component of the overall composite, which makes the near-zero left panel more striking, not less. Michigan and Minnesota are the notable trade-off breakers on the right panel; Tennessee's Access to Capital, Education, and Cost of Living ranks were unavailable in the source table but are not used in either chart.
